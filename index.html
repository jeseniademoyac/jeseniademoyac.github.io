THE COST OF PURE WATER
Life experience of NYC Latinos in search for drinking water under their cultural perceptions.
By: Jesenia De Moya C.

Yadiris Marte, 42, is a Dominican mother of three. During the first three days of the month, she visits the nearest supermarket -across the street- to buy groceries for the next 30 days, including four pack of 16.9 full onzes of water bottles with 40 units each. "These are $9.56. It's cheap!", Marte replies on the cost of each pack she uses to prepare meals and to drink at home.

Although New York is ranked as one of the Top 10 cities with the best tap water supplies nation wide, most Caribbean Latinos don't use it on a daily basis. Some Hispanics consider water to be safer to drink and use when taken out of a recently opened bottle.
